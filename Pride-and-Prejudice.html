" PRIDE OF PREJUDICES "

Overview of Pride and Prejudice

  • Author: Jane Austen
  • Published: 1813
  • Genre: Romantic fiction, comedy of manners
  • Setting: Early 19th-century rural England

Plot Summary

The story follows Elizabeth Bennet, a witty and independent young woman, as she navigates issues of love, class, and societal expectations. Key themes include the importance of marrying for love rather than wealth, personal growth, and overcoming initial judgments. Elizabeth initially misjudges the aloof and wealthy Mr. Darcy as proud and arrogant, while he misjudges her family as unsuitable due to their lower social standing. Over time, both characters confront their flaws—Elizabeth her prejudice and Darcy his pride—and grow closer, ultimately leading to a romantic reconciliation.

Major Characters

  1. Elizabeth Bennet: The intelligent and spirited protagonist.
  2. Fitzwilliam Darcy: A wealthy gentleman with a reserved and proud demeanor.
  3. Jane Bennet: Elizabeth’s kind and gentle elder sister.
  4. Charles Bingley: Darcy’s amiable and wealthy best friend.
  5. Mr. Bennet: Elizabeth’s sarcastic and indifferent father.
  6. Mrs. Bennet: Elizabeth’s frivolous and marriage-obsessed mother.
  7. George Wickham:A charming but deceitful militia officer.
  8. Lady Catherine de Bourgh: Darcy’s overbearing aunt.
